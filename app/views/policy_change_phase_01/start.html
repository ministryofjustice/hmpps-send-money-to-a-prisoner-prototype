Service change from November 2nd

This service change will mean the best way to send money to someone in prison will be by debit card.

You will no longer be able to:

  • send money by bank transfer
  • send in cash, cheques or postal orders by post

This change is to keep everyone who lives and works in prisons safe.
If it means you can no longer use this service, you can get help.

You can use this service to make a payment by Visa, Mastercard or Maestro debit card. Money usually takes less than 3 working days to reach a prisoner’s account, but it may take longer.

This service is free and secure, and available in all prisons in England and Wales.

Start now

Before you start

You’ll need the:

  • prisoner’s date of birth
  • prisoner number

Help using the service

You can get help with making a payment, ask a question about a payment you’ve already made, or ask something else about the service.

Other ways to send money

You can also use the service to find out how to make a UK bank transfer to a prisoner by:

  • online banking
  • mobile banking app
  • telephone banking

If you do not have a bank account

You can also send a postal order, cheque or cash to a prisoner.

Service change from November 2nd

A service change on November 2nd will mean the best way to send money to someone in prison will be by debit card.

You will no longer be able to:

  • send money by bank transfer
  • send in cash, cheques or postal orders by post

This change is to keep everyone who lives and works in prisons safe.
If it means you can no longer use this service, you can get help.

Sending money

Until November 2nd, you can send money to someone in prison in a number of ways.

Debit card

You can make an online payment by Visa, Mastercard and Maestro debit card.

If you cannot use a debit card

You can transfer money by:

  • online banking
  • mobile banking app
  • telephone banking

You’ll need to get bank details and a reference for the prisoner you want to send money to. Include the reference when you transfer the money.

Cheques, postal orders and cash

Cheques or postal orders (payable to ‘NOMS Agency’) are more secure but you can also send cash.

You should address your envelope to the governor of the prison and your letter should include:

  • your name and address
  • the prisoner’s name and prison number

The money is then paid into the prisoner’s account - a basic cash account they can use to send and receive money.
